[<img class="alignright size-full wp-image-165" title="FET" src="https://i2.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/fet11.png?resize=217%2C110" alt="" width="217" height="110" data-recalc-dims="1" />](https://i2.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/fet11.png)Our group creates monolayer and nanoscale transistors with designed defects: both in terms of the type and concentration of defects. The combined experimental and computational research will enable greater understanding of charge transport in molecular materials.

#### **<span style="color: #800000;">Experiment</span>**

[<img class="alignleft" title="3Probe" src="https://i2.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/3probe11.jpg?resize=239%2C150" alt="" width="239" height="150" data-recalc-dims="1" />](https://i2.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/3probe11.jpg)We synthesize a range of _inorganic_ semiconducting complexes, such as metal phthalocyanines and metal terpyridine derivatives.Â By changing the metal ions, we can tailor the relative electronic structure and concentrations of intentional deisgner defects.Â We fabricate our own chips and perform detailed electrical characterization of the materials.

#### **<span style="color: #800000;">Theory</span>**

[<img class="alignright" title="TrapB" src="https://i1.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/trapb11.png?resize=253%2C148" alt="" width="253" height="148" data-recalc-dims="1" />](https://i1.wp.com/pre.hutchison.chem.pitt.edu/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/trapb11.png)Our goal is to scale down our experimental transistors and scale up our simulations to provide a 1:1 correspondence between experiment and theory, benefiting both communities.Â So far, our code can easily handle micron-scale devices.Â We have demonstrated the exact distribution of charged and neutral defects in realistic transistors and the effect of Coulomb interactions on carrier trajectories.Â Results correlate extremely well with experiments.
